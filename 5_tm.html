Transaction Monitoring


Introduction:

Transaction Monitoring (TM) refers to the process of tracking and analyzing financial transactions such as deposits, withdrawals and transfers to first identify it and then to prevent suspicious or fraudulent activities.


To understand it better, we can break down the word. First one is transaction, it refers to a business that is done between 2 or more parties. If you deposit or withdraw the cash then it will be between you and the bank and if you transfer it to anyone then it will be between you, the other person and the bank.


Next word is monitoring. It means the act of observing something. It also means along with observing to keep a record of something. As per Oxford dictionary it means, to watch and check something over a period of time in order to see how it develops.


So, if we merge these 2 meanings, it will be like this: observing/tracking the transaction and keeping a record of it to see if it changes in the future.


The aim of the TM is to detect any unusual pattern that is not matching with the record of the customer that might lead us to money laundering, fraud, or terrorist financing.


The purpose of TM is to detect fraud or money laundering or terrorist and in turn help financial institution to mitigate financial and reputational risk.

Role of Machine Learning in TM:


By now you would have understood that it's not manual task of tracking each and every transaction for unusual transaction, instead it's done by a system that do all the checks. Machine Learning helps create a pattern out of the data that is feeded to it's system and based on that pattern of the data, it starts triggering the alert(s). Apart from learning the pattern from the data, it also keep evolving by adding new pattern (of course, not suspicious data).


With the help of ML, Financial Institution see less number of incorrect triggers, which is referred as false positive in Compliance. It also helps to track real time data rather than checking only historical data. It also helps in creating dynamic customer risk profile based on account behavior, transaction history, demographic information etc.


Apart from all these visible benefits, even most of the regulatory authority encourage and emphasize usage of advanced technologies to enforce and strengthen Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) measures.
