Screening


Introduction:

While KYC focuses on verifying customer identity and assessing risks, screening helps to find if the customer is involved in any illegal activity(ies). Screening is a process adopted by the financial institution to thoroughly examine the individuals or entities against various databases, watchlists, and other relevant sources like Google search engine to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements and to identify potential risks associated with money laundering, terrorism financing, or other illicit activities.


The main purpose of the screening is to mitigate the risk associated with the individual or the entity after identifying and assessing the risk of the customer involvement in any illegal activity.


Are there any types of screening?

Yes, Screening is majorly categories in 2 types:


  1. Name Screening:

    Name screening means screening the names of the customer(s) against certain list like sanction list, watchlist etc. This is done to identify individuals or entities associated with criminal or prohibited activities.


    This is again sub divided into 3 categories:

    1. Sanction Screening: The main objective of sanction screening is to identify and prevent transactions involving sanctioned individuals, organizations, or nations. Sanction screening is checking individual(s)/entity(ies) against sanctions list to ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.

    2. PEP Screening: Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening is to identify and manage risk associated with PEP individuals.This specific screening related to PEP is done as PEP individuals are more prone to corruption, bribery, and misuse of public office for personal gain.

    3. Adverse Media Screening: The primary objective of adverse media screening to screen the individual/entity in open search and to find if there are any adverse/negative news about the individual/entity reported by any legitimate website. Point to note here is majority of the FIs do not consider allegations rather than they focus only on proved one.

  2. Transaction Screening:

    This is another type of screening wherein instead of screening customer, FIs screen financial transaction for unusual activities. If you would have read TM article then you might be thinking, ok this is similar to TM then why separate name. There are difference between transaction monitoring and transaction screeening, one of the major difference is generally, TM monitors both historical as well as real time basis whereas transaction screening generally happens real time to immediately stop any unusual activity.


So, where this database of watchlist or sanction list comes from?


Every Government maintains their own sanction list (Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), European Union (EU) Sanctions List), then we can get information on PEP (FATF PEP List, World-Check PEP Database), there are FIUs (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN), Financial Intelligence Unit-India (FIU-IND)), Global databases (Interpol's Global Databases, World-Check Database,Thomson Reuters Screening Database) and there are open search through whcih we get details about any adverse media on entities and individuals etc.
