Funds


Introduction:


A fund is a pool of money that is allocated for a specific purpose.

The pool of money in a fund is often invested and professionally managed in order to generate returns for its investors.


Types of Fund:

There are many types of funds. We will briefly discuss few of them here:


  1. Mutual Funds: This is one of the most common type of funds these days. It is a type of fund wherein it collects money from multiple investors and invest in diversified portfolio mainly into stocks and bonds.

    Every investor cannot take different decision of where to invest money like which stock or bond, instead an individual is appointed to take those decision, who is called fund manager. He is the one who make investment decisions on behalf of the investors based on the fund's objectives.

    The expense ratio represents the annual fees and operating expenses expressed as a percentage of the fund's average net assets. It includes management fees, administrative costs, and other expenses.


    • Open-End Mutual Funds: Investors can buy and sell shares at the net asset value (NAV) per share.
    • Closed-End Mutual Funds: A fixed number of shares are issued, and these shares trade on an exchange like stocks.

  2. Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs): As the name itself suggests these gets traded on stock exchange. This is similar to Mutual funds, however there is one major difference between them. ETFs are bought and sold just like normal stock but MFs are bought at the end of the trading day.

    ETFs often have lower expense ratios compared to traditional mutual funds.

    This is also managed by fund manager, who takes decision on behalf of investors.


  3. Hedge Funds: Hedge funds are investment funds that pool capital from accredited individuals or institutional investors to employ various strategies in the financial markets with the goal of generating high returns.

    This fund is generally open to HNI (High Networth Individuals) & institutional investors as there is a high minimum investment requirements.

    This type of fund is more flexible as they can invest across different asset classes, such as equities, fixed income, currencies, commodities, and real estate.

    Hedge fund managers charge two types of fees, management fee (a percentage of assets under management) and a performance fee (a percentage of the profits earned).


  4. Private Equity Funds: Private equity funds are investment funds that pool capital from institutional and high-net-worth individual investors to invest in privately held companies as well as controlling stake in publicly traded companies.

    Unlike any other investment fund, this type of fund focus on long term investments, generally for 5 to 10 years.

    This fund typically takes equity ownership in the companies so that they can have influence over strategic decisions and management.

    They are structured as limited partnership wherein the fund manager acting as the general partner and investors as Limited partners.

    There investment strategies focuses on venture capital (early-stage companies), growth equity (expanding companies), and buyouts (acquiring a controlling interest in established companies).


  5. Real Estate Investment Trusts: As the name itself suggests, it aims at investing in real estate. are investment vehicles that own, operate, or finance income-generating real estate across various sectors.

    In this fund, an investor gain exposure to real estate assets without directly owning or managing the properties.

    They are traded on stock exchanges so unlike physical real estate they are liquid in nature.


    1. Equity REITs: Own and operate income-generating real estate properties. Rental income and capital appreciation are primary sources of revenue.

    2. Mortgage REITs (mREITs): Invest in or originate mortgage loans and mortgage-backed securities. Income is generated through interest on mortgage loans.

    3. Hybrid REITs: Combine characteristics of both equity and mortgage REITs by owning properties and investing in mortgages.

  6. Pension Funds: A pension fund is a type of investment fund that is set up by employers, governments, or other organizations to provide income for employees upon retirement.

    In this fund, both employers and employees invest with the goal of generating returns and building a pool of assets to fund retirement benefits.

    As this fund is invested with the intension of retirement benefit, the investment will be for long term.


    1. Defined Benefit (DB) Pension Plans: Provides a specific amount to employees upon retirement, considering factors like salary and years of service. In this case, the employer faces the risk.

    2. Defined Contribution (DC) Pension Plans: Contributions made by both employers and employees will be specified, by keeping in mind that the retirement benefit is dependent on the investment performance. In this case, the employees takes the risk.

  7. Sovereign Wealth Funds: Sovereign Wealth Fund (SWF) is a state-owned investment fund. That means the investment generally comes from the country's surplus reserves.

    They invest in almost everything like stocks, bonds, private equity, metals, real estate, hedge funds.

    SWFs typically have a long-term investment focus and are not subject to short-term market pressures.

    SWFs can have geopolitical implications, as their investment decisions may influence global financial markets, industries, and diplomatic relations.


  8. Money Market Funds: Money Market Funds (MMFs) are short-term and low risk funds. This is popular as it becomes an alternative to investors savings account.

    They focus on highly liquid instruments like Certificates of Deposits, Treasury bills, Short-term Government debts, Commercial paper etc.


    1. Government Money Market Funds: As the name suggest, it invest in Government securities which makes it safe investment.
    2. Municipal Money Market Funds: Their focus will be on short-term municipal securities.
    3. Prime Money Market Funds: Invest in a other range of short-term instruments, including commercial papers.

Related Parties (General):

  • Fund Manager
  • Investor(s), if their contribution constitute high percentage
  • Fund Sponsor
  • General Partner/Limited Partner/Trustee, depending on the type of fund
  • Custodian
