Limited liability partnership (LLP)


Introduction:


Limited liability partnership (LLP) is a type of general partnership where every partner has a limited personal liability for the debts of the partnership.


One of the primary advantages of an LLP is that partners are not personally liable for the debts and liabilities of the LLP. LLPs allow limited liability even if partners remain involved in the management of the business.


For example, Mr A and Mr B have opened a restaturant and due to some reason people fell sick because of this resturant food, then they cannot both be personally sued for damages as their liability will be limited.


Related Parties (General):

  • Designated partners
  • Ultimate Beneficial Owner(s)
  • Intermediate Beneficial Owner(s)
  • Signatories
  • Director(s), only in some cases
