Bank Secrecy Act


Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) is a US law that was introduced in 1970, with the purpose to prevent and detect money laundering and terrorist financing. It does this by implementing various records and reporting requirements on financial institutions. Reporting requirement like for transactions over $10,000 per day should be reported as CTR etc.


Another name for BSA is CFTRA, Currency and Foreign Transactions Reporting Act. It is generally used by the IRS and the FBI to investigate as well as prevent any illegal financial activities.


What are the reporting requirements under BSA?


Following are the reporting requirements under BSA:


  • Currency Transaction Report (CTR): The financial institutions should report CTR when there is cash transaction exceeding $10,000 in single day irrespective of whether it's done in one single transaction or multiple transactions.
  • Suspicious Activity Report (SAR/STR): FIs must report SAR when they notice that their client is trying to avoid reporting requirement of CTR by transacting just below $10,000. It also comes into picture when the FIs find any unusual activity or suspicious activity found in the customer account.
  • Foreign Bank Account Report (FBAR): United States citizens and residents with a financial interest in or authority over foreign bank accounts with an aggregate value of $10,000 or more are required to file a FBAR with the U.S. Treasury.
  • Monetary Instrument Log (MIL): MIL is a record where FIs are required to keep details of the customers where they buy monetary instruments such as cashier's check, money orders etc., but only if they are valued between $3,000 to $10,000. If it's le3ss than $3,000 then they can avoid to report it.
  • Currency and Monetary Instrument Report (CMIR), also known as The "Report of International Transportation of Currency or Monetary Instruments", is used to report physical transportation of currency or monetary instrument in and out of United States. It is worth noting here that the value should be more than $10,000.
