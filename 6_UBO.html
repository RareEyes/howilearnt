Ultimate Beneficial Owner


Introduction:

Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) refers to the natural person who ultimately owns or controls a legal entity. A UBO or Ultimate Beneficial Owner is the person that is the ultimate beneficiary when an institution initiates a transaction. To designate an individual as the Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO), they must possess a specific percentage of ownership in an entity, either through shares or rights. The calculation of this percentage is contingent upon the risk rating assigned to the company. Typically, the company adheres to the following approach:


Risk Rating Percentage
Low 25% or more
Medium 25% or more
High 10% or more

How to calculate ownership structure:


Identifying the ultimate beneficial owner is crucial for various reasons, including transparency, preventing money laundering, and ensuring compliance with regulations. The process of determining the ultimate beneficial owner may involve investigating and disclosing the ownership structure of a company or other legal entity. It is an essential part of Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance efforts.

Intermediary Beneficial Owners (IBOs)

Intermediary Beneficial Owners (IBOs) are legal entities that possess shares in another company. IBOs act as intermediaries between a company and its ultimate beneficial owner(s), unless the entity is a listed entity or a government entity. In cases where either the IBO or the company is listed on stock exchanges or is a government entity, most financial institutions typically do not delve further, as such entities are subject to regulatory oversight.
